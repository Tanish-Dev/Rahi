📖 ABOUT SECTION
Heading: Why India?
Body:
India is not just a destination — it is an experience that stays with you forever. From the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas to the serene backwaters of Kerala, from the golden sands of Rajasthan to the lush forests of the Northeast, India is a land of extraordinary contrasts. With over 40 UNESCO World Heritage Sites, a civilisation stretching back thousands of years, and a living culture of festivals, art, cuisine, and spirituality, there is something in India for every kind of traveller.

🌟 FEATURED DESTINATIONS (Cards)
1. Rajasthan — The Royal Desert State
Majestic forts, camel safaris across golden dunes, colourful bazaars, and royal heritage. Visit Jaipur, Jodhpur, Jaisalmer, and Udaipur.
2. Kerala — God's Own Country
Tranquil backwaters, lush tea estates, Ayurvedic retreats, and pristine beaches. Explore Munnar, Alleppey, Kochi, and Kovalam.
3. Ladakh — Land of High Passes
Dramatic moonscapes, ancient monasteries, turquoise lakes, and thrilling mountain roads. A paradise for adventure seekers.
4. Goa — Sun, Sand & Soul
Golden beaches, Portuguese-era churches, vibrant nightlife, and fresh seafood. India's most beloved coastal escape.
5. Varanasi — The Eternal City
One of the world's oldest living cities, with sacred ghats, dawn boat rides on the Ganges, and centuries of spiritual tradition.
6. Hampi — Ruins of an Empire
A UNESCO World Heritage Site, with stunning boulder-strewn landscapes and the majestic ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire.

🌱 RESPONSIBLE TRAVEL (Travel for LiFE)
Heading: Travel Responsibly
India invites you to explore mindfully. Choose eco-friendly accommodations, respect local customs and dress codes at religious sites, support local artisans and village markets, avoid single-use plastics, and leave every destination as beautiful as you found it.

❓ FAQ SECTION
Q: Do I need a visa to visit India?
Most international travellers can apply for an e-Visa online before arrival. Visit the official Indian government e-Visa portal for eligibility and applications.
Q: What is the best time to visit India?
October to March is ideal for most of India. Hill stations are best in summer (April–June). Monsoon (July–September) is magical in Kerala and the Northeast.
Q: Is India safe for solo travellers?
Yes. India has millions of solo travellers every year. It helps to plan ahead, use registered transport, and stay in well-reviewed accommodations.
Q: What currency is used in India?
The Indian Rupee (INR). ATMs are widely available in cities and major tourist spots.
